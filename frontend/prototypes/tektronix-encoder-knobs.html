Design language — lab instrument panels

Late-80s / 90s lab-instrument “scientific brutalism.” Matte beige or cool gray metal or plastic fascia, black hairline rules and color-coded section borders, heavy mechanical depth, and one loud accent (phosphor green on the CRT, or signal red on warnings). Everything reads as tool, not app.

Panels and layout

  • Face material: Putty / “computer beige” or cool light gray, often semi-matte with a faint eggshell or fine texture (painted metal, brushed aluminum, or molded ABS), not glassy UI chrome.
  • Structure: Controls grouped into rectangular cells with thin black outlines; some blocks get accent rails (for example blue for vertical, green for trigger). Color is organizational, not decorative.
  • Density: Tight grids of labels, scales, and warnings; small caps sans labels; red for UNCAL, VARIABLE, max voltage. Red means caution / secondary scale, not a generic error state.
  • Depth: Recessed CRT wells, shadows under knobs and chiclet keys, long mechanical button travel implied by gaps and height.

Screens (CRT)

  • Bezel: Deep rectangular or rounded-rect recess; sometimes a bright ring around a round tube, or a flat graticule on slightly convex glass.
  • Image: Dark field, green trace with bloom; etched or drawn graticule (fine grid, center cross); on-screen legends mimic vector-stroke typography.
  • Readout layer: Small glowing numerics and icons with soft halation, not flat LCD pixels.

Knobs and encoders

  • Dark skirted lab encoders: Black or dark gray cylinder, coarse vertical knurl, flat top with silver or white index line; scale ticks printed on the panel.
  • Small white thermoplastic encoders (Tek-adjacent): Cylindrical flat top, fine molded grip (here: subtle vertical micro-flutes), satin (not mirror) plastic; radial tick marks ride on the rotating cap with the index. Often a minimal metal bezel ring; feels small, heavy, precise.
  • Shared cues: Rotation is the primary interaction; knobs cast short, hard shadows.

Buttons and keys

  • Chiclet / block keys: Light gray or white, slightly proud of the panel, flat or barely domed tops, matte; long mechanical stroke.
  • Soft keys under CRT: Darker gray tabs, rounded lower edge, aligned to on-screen menu columns.

Switches and hardware

  • Slide / paddle switches: Black plastic bats in milled slots, faceted profile, discrete positions.
  • Connectors: BNC — bright nickel/chrome with black plastic nut; binding posts — red and gray, knurled metal. They anchor the panel as real I/O.

Indicators

  • Bubble LEDs: Small hemispherical domes, glass or clear plastic; specular catch on the curve, darker at the skirt; “on” state is localized bloom from the die, not a flat UI pill.

How to reuse this vocabulary

Palette: putty / cool gray / black rules / one phosphor accent (+ signal red for warnings on static labels). Geometry: recessed wells, hairline grids, orthogonal grouping. Materials: matte substrate, satin plastic, brushed metal, chromed hardware — each material gets one highlight model. Motion: translation and rotation with visible depth; reserve glow for emitters (trace, LED, backlit legend), not neutral borders.
